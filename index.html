I am an Assistant Professor at the Department of Physics at MNIT Jaipur (India). My research of interest is non-perturbative Quantum Field Theory. Currently I am exploring non-perturbative aspects of Matrix Models through the resurgence framework. I am also working on strong-field QED where the external fields are large enough to render traditional perturbation theory insufficient.

Education

I did my PhD at University of Connecticut under the great guidance of Prof. Gerald Dunne. My thesis mainly explores the resurgent asymptotics in matrix models, in particular the Gross-Witten-Wadia model. I also did some work on the Gross-Neveu model, a simple fermionic model which shares several important properties with Quantum Chronodynamics.

I did my bachelors and masters at IISER Pune where I was advised by Dr. Arijit Bhattacharyay for my master's thesis. During my studies at IISER Pune, I also did small semester projects with Prof. Sudarshan Ananth and Prof. Avinash Khare, as well as a summer project at IIT Mandi under Prof. Arghya Taraphder.
